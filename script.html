⚡ TOREVEZ CALL SCRIPT

Goal of every call: book a demo or schedule a setup day. 90% are too busy to set up live.
OPENER (DEFAULT)

Use this every time

"Hey, is this the owner? This is Peyton, founder of Torevez. We built a system that when you miss a call, it picks up, talks to the customer, gets their info, and texts you the lead right away. You don't pay until it catches you a paying job."
Short. Ends on the offer. Let them respond.
VOICEMAIL A, OWNER'S CELL

When the owner's voicemail picks up

"Hey, Peyton here, you just missed our call, which is actually exactly what we fix. When a call goes unanswered our system picks it up, gets the customer's info, and texts it to you right away. You don't pay until it catches you a paying job. Call back at [number]."
Under 20 seconds. The "you just missed our call, which is exactly what we fix" line is the hook. Don't ramble after.
VOICEMAIL B, GATEKEEPER OR ANSWERING SERVICE

When a receptionist, wife, or answering service picks up

"Peyton calling from Torevez. We built a system that when a call goes unanswered, it picks up, talks to the customer, and texts the owner the full lead right away. You don't pay until it catches a paying job. Best number to reach me is [number]."
Don't pitch the gatekeeper directly. Just leave the message clean and let them pass it on. If they ask what it's about, repeat the line and add: "5 minute thing, no rush."
REAL GOAL OF EVERY CALL

90% won't set up live, that's fine

Most owners are on a roof, in a truck, or between jobs when you reach them. They are not setting up software in that moment, no matter how good the offer is.
The win is:
1. A 10-minute demo booked on the calendar (best)
2. A specific setup time on the calendar (second best)
3. A scheduled follow-up call within 7 days (third best)
If you walk away from a call with a date and time on the calendar, you won. If you walk away with "send me info" or "I'll think about it," you lost. Push for a date every time.
OBJECTIONS

Short answers, no lectures

"I already track bookings in a notebook/calendar/spreadsheet."
"Yeah, and you're doing all the data entry yourself, plus you forget half the reminders. With this, a customer books at 9pm Sunday and by the time you wake up Monday it's on your calendar with reminders already set for both of you. You just check the app."
This is the booking-pain objection. Most owners think they have it handled with manual tracking. The unlock is showing them the COST of doing it manually (time, forgotten reminders, missed jobs from double-bookings), not the feature itself.
"Is that AI? I don't want a robot answering my customers."
"I get it, most AI bots sound terrible. Mine's trained specifically for HVAC calls and sounds like a real person. Customers don't realize it isn't until they're already booked. And you only pay if it actually catches you jobs, so if it sounds bad, you stop and you don't owe me anything."
"What's the catch?"
"No catch. I need real HVAC shops running it so I know it works in the field. Free until it catches you a paying job. Only doing this for the first 5 companies."
"How much after?"
"$50/month for the missed-call catcher, locked for life as a founding customer. One caught job covers a year. Standard rate after the 5 founders is $75."
"I'm too busy to set this up right now."
"Totally fair, that's why I'm not asking you to do it on this call. Let's just put 10 minutes on the calendar this week, I'll walk you through it, and if it makes sense we set it up then. What day works better, Thursday or Friday?"
This is the most common objection. The answer turns it into a calendar booking, which is the actual goal.
"How do I know it works?"
"You don't pay until it proves it does. That's the deal."
"I already use [X]."
"Cool, does it answer when you're under a unit at 7pm? What's it cost you a month?"
Their answer to the cost question is the whole game. If they're paying $200+/month for something that misses calls, your $0-until-it-works offer sells itself. Let them say the number out loud.
"Send me info / email me."
"Yeah I can. Real quick though, are you actually interested or just being polite? No offense, I just don't want to waste your time or mine."
If "actually interested" then book the demo right now. If "being polite" then thank them and end the call. The email rarely converts.
"Not interested."
"Fair. Real quick, is it because you don't miss calls, or because you've tried something like this before?"
If "don't miss calls" then "Most owners I talk to are surprised when they actually track it. Want me to send a free missed-call check tool?" If "tried before" then "Got it, what didn't work about the last one?"
CLOSE (3 OPTIONS, NO 4TH)

When they're warm, give them a real choice

Option 1 (best): "Let's put a 10-minute demo on the calendar. I'll walk you through it, you see it work, you decide. What's better, [Day A] morning or [Day B] afternoon?"
Option 2: "Or if you're already convinced, let's schedule 15 minutes for the setup. I'll do everything, you just forward your line. What day this week works?"
Option 3: "Or, if the timing's off this week, I'll save your founding spot until [date 5 days out] and check back. After that I have to release it to the next owner."
Three options forces a decision. Demo / setup / save spot. All three put a date on the calendar. Anything that isn't one of those three is a soft no.
If they pick option 1 or 2: grab cell + best email + carrier. Send calendar invite same hour.
THE OFFER (REFERENCE)

What you're actually selling

A missed-call system that picks up every call you can't. Gets the customer's info. Texts you the lead in 10 seconds. You pay nothing until it catches you a paying job. Setup takes 5 minutes.
Founding pricing (only first 5 customers):
Missed-call catcher: $50/month locked for life. Standard rate after: $75/month.
Full system (call catcher + booking + follow-ups): $75/month locked for life. Standard rate after: $150/month.
Default pitch is the missed-call tier. Lowest barrier, clearest pain. Upsell to full system AFTER they see it work, not on the first call.
Don't say "AI" unprompted. Owners hear AI and think cheap chatbot. Describe what it DOES, not what it is.

Call Day Rules

1. Tuesday & Thursday only. 15 dials each (Week 1), ramping to 30 over 5 weeks.
2. The win is a date on the calendar, not a setup on the call. Push for demo/setup/follow-up time, not on-the-spot yes.
3. Don't pitch tiers, features, or roadmap. One offer: pay-only-when-it-works, $50 missed-call founding.
4. Log every call before dialing the next: # dialed, # answered, # demos booked, # objections.
5. If you dread the call: 5 deep breaths and dial. The dread leaves 90 seconds in.
6. Three-option close every time. Demo / setup / save spot. No yes/no questions at the end.
